Welcome to Kentucky!

Explore the beauty and excitement of the Bluegrass State.



Kentucky is a state located in the southeastern part of the United States, bordered by Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, West Virginia, Virginia, Tennessee, and Missouri. The state is renowned for its rolling bluegrass-covered hills, horse farms, and the mighty Ohio River. It has a rich history, having played a significant role in the development of the southern United States.

Lake Cumberland, one of Kentucky's gems, is a vast reservoir nestled in the scenic Appalachian foothills. With over 1,200 miles of shoreline, it's a haven for water enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy a variety of water activities, including boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake's clear waters and surrounding greenery make it an ideal destination for relaxation and outdoor recreation.

Kentucky is internationally acclaimed for its bourbon production. The state is home to numerous distilleries, each with its unique take on this iconic American whiskey. Bourbon enthusiasts can embark on a bourbon trail, visiting distilleries like Maker's Mark, Buffalo Trace, and Woodford Reserve. Guided tours and tastings provide an insight into the bourbon-making process, its history, and, of course, the opportunity to savor the distinct flavors of Kentucky bourbon.

Kentucky is synonymous with thoroughbred horse racing and host the most exciting 2 mins in sports history with the Kentucky Derby! The picturesque landscapes, especially around areas like Lexington, offer scenic trails for riders of all skill levels. Horse enthusiasts can explore the rolling hills and expansive horse farms, immersing themselves in the equestrian culture that defines Kentucky.

Whether you're exploring the beatiful shores of Lake Cumberland, indulging in bourbon tastings, experiencing the thrill of the Kentucky Derby, or immersing yourself in the natural beauty of hiking trails, Kentucky promises a unique and memorable experience for every visitor.
